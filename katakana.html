Katakana

Katakana is one of Japanese's syllabaries.

A syllabary is a collection of characters that all represent a syllable's sound.

In English, we have an alphabet. But, most of the "letters" in Japanese represent a syllable, not a singular phonetic.

Katakana is reserved for words not native to the Japanese language.

Words like おはよう (good morning) are native to Japanese, so they're written in Hirgana.

Words like コーヒー (coffee) are not native to Japanese, so they're written in Katakana.
